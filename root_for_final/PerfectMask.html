Project Decsription

This project was a way to teach students to be familiar with Photoshops layer masking function. Inspired by the artist John Baldessari, the aim was to highlight interesting silhouettes using a masking layer.
I found two black and white photographs, keeping in mind that the silhouettes would need to be in recognizable positions to be effective. I discovered an image of a boy readying himself to throw a snowball, and another of Andy Warhol and two other figures positioned around a manhole. This assignment didn't give me any trouble, however the work was somewhat tedious trying to achieve clean masking layers.


Perfect Mask #1

I created this image by creating a masking layer, obscuring the boy holding a snowball, and including the background of dirty snow. I picked the color red for this silhouette, juxtaposing violence and innocence.


Perfect Mask #2

This image was created using the same method as the first, but obscuring the background rather than the subjects of the photo. I chose violet to fill the background for no particular reason, simply because I thought it fit well with the tone of the image.
